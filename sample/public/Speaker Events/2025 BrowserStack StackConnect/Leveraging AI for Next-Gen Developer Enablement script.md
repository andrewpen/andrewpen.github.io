# **StackConnect Presentation Script & Talk Track**

Presenter: Andrew Pendleton  
Time Allotment: 20-25 minutes  
Audience: Digital Experience & Testing Professionals  
Core Theme: Quality is the most direct path to sustainable productivity.

### **Part 1: The Hook \- The Productivity Trap (2-3 minutes)**

**(On Screen: Slide 1 \- Title Slide: "Leveraging AI for Next-Gen Developer Enablement")**

"Good afternoon, everyone. My name is Andrew Pendleton, and I lead the Design Systems team for the consumer group at Verizon.

It's great to be here at StackConnect. For the past year, our entire industry has been completely captivated by AI. And if you read the headlines, it seems like we're all chasing a single metric: Productivity. How much faster can we ship? How many more lines of code can we write? We see incredible stats... 70%, 80% increases in speed."

**(CLICK to Slide 20 \- "IT'S A TRAP")**

"...But I'm here today to make a case that a blind focus on productivity is a trap. Our experience at Verizon has taught us that chasing speed for speed's sake often leads to tech debt, inconsistent experiences, and a decline in quality.

My core message today is this: a deliberate, rigorous focus on *quality* is the most direct, most sustainable path to meaningful productivity gains. Today, I'm going to share how we're building that quality-first future with AI at Verizon."

### **Part 2: Our Foundation for Quality at Scale \- VDS (3-4 minutes)**

**(CLICK through Slides 4-6 \- Intro to VDS)**

"So, how do you even begin to enforce quality across an organization the size of Verizon? We have thousands of developers and designers working on countless digital experiences. Building consistently at that scale is incredibly hard.

To solve for this, you need a centralized foundation. For us, that's the Verizon Design System, or VDS."

**(PAUSE on Slide 7 \- The Four Pillars)**

"VDS is more than just a component library. It’s a complete ecosystem built on four key pillars: Quality, Consistency, Inclusivity, and Efficiency.

These aren't just marketing buzzwords. For us, these are the essential guardrails. They ensure that as we integrate AI to move faster, we don't sacrifice the quality and trust our customers expect. They are the bedrock of our strategy."

### **Part 3: The Core \- Supercharging the Testing Lifecycle with AI (8-10 minutes)**

**(CLICK to Slide 14 \- "Intelligent automation of visual testing...")**

"With that foundation in place, we can apply AI with surgical precision. And for this audience, I want to focus on the area where we believe AI has the most profound impact: **Automating Testing and Quality Assurance.**

This is the heart of our strategy. Let's break down a few key initiatives.

**(Advance to Slide 15 for visual support)**

First, **Advanced Visual Regression Testing.** Standard pixel-by-pixel comparisons are noisy. They flag every minor rendering difference. We're using AI to develop 'Smart Diffing'—it understands the structure of a component and can differentiate between a critical bug and an insignificant anti-aliasing difference. It's about reducing noise so our teams can focus on what matters.

And of course, testing has to happen everywhere. To ensure our components render perfectly across every experience, our pipeline relies on a comprehensive suite of tools, **including Browserstack**, to validate against thousands of device and browser combinations automatically.

Next is **Accessibility.** We're automating baseline checks—color contrast, ARIA attributes, keyboard navigation—directly in our CI/CD pipeline. The vision isn't just to flag issues, but to move towards suggesting, and eventually implementing, automated remediation for common problems.

Finally, we're shifting quality all the way to the left with the **VDS Guardian Angel.** This is an AI-powered companion inside Figma. It doesn't get in the way, but it quietly observes and surfaces when a designer uses a deprecated component, or a color combination that fails accessibility standards. It's proactive QA, preventing entire classes of defects from ever being created."

### **Part 4: Supporting Quality Upstream & Downstream (5-6 minutes)**

**(Verbally reference Slide 8 \- The Iceberg Metaphor)**

"Now, that deep, complex testing work is the massive part of the iceberg below the surface. That’s where the revolutionary change is happening. But we also use AI at the 'tip of the iceberg' to support quality both upstream and downstream from development."

**(CLICK through Slides 10 & 11 \- Intelligent Code Generation)**

"Let's look at **Intelligent Code Generation.** On the surface, this looks like a productivity feature. But we see it as a *quality feature*.

When our AI generates the boilerplate for a new component, it's not just about saving a developer 20 minutes. It's about ensuring every single component starts its life from a perfect, pre-tested, accessible, and brand-compliant template. We eliminate human error at the source."

**(CLICK through Slides 12 & 13 \- Intelligent Workflows)**

"The same philosophy applies to **Intelligent Workflows.** By embedding AI into tools like Slack and Jira, we can do things like perform real-time brand compliance audits on marketing copy or even help synthesize user research from customer calls. This ensures we're not just building the product right, but that we're building the *right product* in the first place."

### **Part 5: The Conclusion \- Purposeful Productivity (2 minutes)**

**(CLICK to Slides 17-19 \- The "Productivity with a Purpose" stats)**

"So what happens when you stop chasing productivity and start chasing quality?

The productivity becomes a natural, sustainable outcome. It turns out, when you give people tools that make it easy and fast to do the right thing, you see incredible gains like these. These numbers aren't the goal—they are the *result* of a quality-first mindset."

**(CLICK to the final Takeaway Slides 22 & 23\)**

"So, as you head out today, I want to leave you with three things to remember.

First, an AI-powered design system is more than a library; it's a dynamic **quality and productivity engine.**

Second, while code generation is cool, the most meaningful AI applications are often the deep, complex ones **below the surface**—the ones focused on testing and validation.

And finally, don't fall into the productivity trap. **Quality is the true engine of sustainable speed.**

Thank you."
